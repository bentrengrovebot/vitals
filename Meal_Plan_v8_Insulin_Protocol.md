# Meal Plan v8 — Insulin Resistance Protocol

**Created:** 8 April 2026
**Purpose:** Aggressive sustainable fat loss to reverse insulin resistance (fasting insulin 126 pmol/L), lower apoB, protect liver, restore testosterone. Every macro decision serves the metabolic goal.

**Sources:** Attia (metabolic/insulin framework), Israetel (protein/training nutrition), Avena + Fogg (trigger food elimination, environment design), D'Agostino (carb restriction for NAFLD reversal)

---

## The Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Protein floor: 200g/day.** Never drops. This preserves muscle, maximises thermic effect, controls appetite.
2. **Carbs: 75g/day.** No starchy carbs (rice, potato, bread, pasta, crackers) before lunch. Starchy carbs exist ONLY at post-training lunch and family dinner. Incidental carbs from cottage cheese, carrots, almonds, and vegetables are already counted in the plan — these are fine.
3. **Fat: the only lever that moves.** Steps down across weeks as the deficit tightens.
4. **Fat per meal: stay at or below 20g.** Enzyme capacity constraint. Take Thorne digestive enzyme with lunch and dinner.
5. **USANA shake is gone.** The maltodextrin is a direct insulin spike. Dropped permanently.
6. **No ultra-processed food enters the house.** Purchasing rule, not willpower rule.
7. **Kitchen closed after Meal 5.** Hard boundary. 500ml water + 20-minute wait if cravings hit.
8. **Weigh protein and starch sources.** Every time. The 5 foods that matter: meat/chicken, cottage cheese, rice, potato, almonds.

## MEAL 4 — 5:30pm Family Dinner (CARB WINDOW 2)

**Take Thorne digestive enzyme with this meal.**

Your partner cooks, the meals vary. You don't control the menu. You control portions. Here's the template:

### The Plate Rule

1. **PROTEIN: Weigh 200–250g of the cooked protein.** Whatever it is — chicken, mince, lamb, fish. Put it on a plate. That's your protein. Target: ~45–55P.
2. **STARCH: Palm of hand, roughly 60–75g cooked.** Rice, potato, kumara. If it's a pasta night, take a half-fist of pasta — or skip it and fill with extra veg. Target: ~16–20C.
3. **VEGETABLES: Fill the rest of the plate.** Whatever's there. Target: ~5–7C.
4. **SAUCE: 1 tablespoon maximum.** Measure it, don't pour. Target: ~2–3C.
5. **No seconds. Ever.**

### Dinner Scenarios That Need Rules

| Scenario | What to do |

| **Pasta night** | Skip pasta. Take the sauce + protein + veg. Or take 50g cooked pasta max (~14C). |

## Tracking System: 3 Numbers Only

You built an app. Use it. But only track three things:

1. **Protein (grams)** — target 200
2. **Carbs (grams)** — target 75
3. **Total calories** — target per your current week

**What to weigh:** meat/chicken, cottage cheese, rice/potato, almonds. That's it. Don't weigh vegetables, don't weigh cooking oil (use a measured tablespoon), don't weigh sauce (use a measured tablespoon).

**When to log:** immediately after each meal. Not at end of day. 30 seconds per entry.

**Weekly review:** every Sunday evening. If you logged 5+ days, that's a win. If 3 or fewer, something in the system broke — identify the friction and fix it.

## Emotional Eating Protocol (Avena + Fogg)

This plan removes decisions. But the vulnerability window is 8pm–10pm after kitchen closes.

**If a craving hits:**
1. 500ml water. Drink it all.
2. Set a 20-minute timer.
3. If still hungry after 20 minutes: Meal 5 (if not already eaten) or 200g cottage cheese.
4. If it's not hunger but emotion: name the trigger briefly ("stressed about work", "bored", "tired") and walk away. The craving will peak and pass within 15–20 minutes.

**There is no moderation with trigger foods.** If it's in the house, it gets eaten. The rule is: it doesn't enter the house. This is a purchasing decision, not an eating decision.
