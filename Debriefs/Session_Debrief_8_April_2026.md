## Statin Research Synthesis

### Experts Reviewed

| Expert | Role | Position on Statins |
|--------|------|---------------------|
| Peter Attia | Longevity physician | Pro-pharmacology, takes 3 lipid drugs (no statin), wants apoB as low as possible |
| Tom Dayspring | Lipidologist | Pro-pharmacology, low-dose statin + alternatives, monitors desmosterol |
| Nick Norwitz | Lipid researcher | Nuanced — flags statin side effects (GLP-1 suppression, mitochondrial harm), prefers PCSK9i |
| Ford Brewer | Preventive cardiologist | Low-dose rosuvastatin only if plaque confirmed, reversed own plaque |
| Aseem Malhotra | NHS cardiologist | Statin-sceptical, published BMJ papers, frames benefit as marginal |
| Mark Hyman | Functional medicine | Metabolic disease is root cause, cholesterol is bystander, pro-testing not pro-statin |
| Dr. Alex | UK emergency medicine | Balanced — LDL/apoB causal, statins one tool among many, expectations key |
| Eric Westman | Obesity medicine | Effect size overstated, lifestyle/keto may rival statins, GLP-1s emerging alternative |
| Pradip Jamnadas | Interventional cardiologist | Small dense LDL is the problem, not total LDL. Root causes: IR, gut LPS, toxins, omega-6 |
| Paul Saladino | MD/PhD | LDL not concerning if metabolically healthy (Framingham HDL stratification). Fix insulin sensitivity |
| Dr. Sil Dan | General practice | Statin-sceptical, wants CAC + HOMA-IR before prescribing |

### Universal Agreements (Every Expert)

1. **Insulin resistance is the primary or co-primary driver of cardiovascular disease.** No expert disputes this.
2. **Standard LDL alone is a poor predictor.** All favour apoB, particle testing, or metabolic context over basic LDL.
3. **Low-dose statins only if statins at all.** 85% of efficacy at quarter dose (5mg rosuvastatin). Nobody advocates high-dose.
4. **Lifestyle is foundational.** Every expert — pro-statin or anti-statin — agrees medications cannot replace fat loss, training, nutrition, sleep, and stress management.
5. **Atorvastatin is not the preferred statin.** Rosuvastatin or pitavastatin preferred by those who use statins at all. Atorvastatin linked to worst insulin resistance, GLP-1 suppression, and mitochondrial damage.
6. **CAC scoring is underutilised and informative.** Tells you if disease is present, which changes the risk calculus.
7. **Alternatives to statins now exist** that didn't 10-20 years ago: ezetimibe, bempedoic acid, PCSK9 inhibitors.

### Key Divergences

**Is LDL/apoB inherently causal for atherosclerosis?**
- Pro-causal (Attia, Alex, Dayspring): Mendelian randomisation, multiple drug classes reducing events, mechanistic plausibility
- Sceptical (Malhotra, Saladino, Jamnadas, Hyman): Relationship disappears in metabolically healthy, half of MI patients have normal LDL, absolute benefit is tiny, it's damaged/small dense LDL not native LDL

**Practical implication for Ben:** This debate is less relevant because Ben is NOT metabolically healthy. Even the sceptics would flag his profile as high-risk given his insulin resistance, high trigs, low HDL, and likely small dense LDL pattern.

**Should primary prevention patients use pharmacology?**
- Attia: Yes, aggressively, especially with elevated Lp(a)
- Brewer: Only if plaque confirmed on imaging (CAC/CIMT/CTA)
- Malhotra/Sil Dan: Rarely — lifestyle first, absolute benefit is marginal
- Norwitz: If needed, prefer PCSK9 inhibitors or non-statin options

### Non-Statin Pharmacological Options

| Drug | Mechanism | Pros | Cons | NZ Availability |
|------|-----------|------|------|-----------------|
| **Ezetimibe** | Blocks cholesterol reabsorption in gut | Cheap, no metabolic side effects, no brain/muscle effects | Less potent (~15-20% apoB reduction as monotherapy) | Available, funded |
| **Bempedoic acid** | Cholesterol synthesis inhibitor (liver only, prodrug) | No muscle/brain effects, no diabetes risk | Expensive, may not be funded in NZ | Check availability |
| **PCSK9 inhibitors** | Prevents LDL receptor degradation on liver | Most potent, essentially zero side effects, lowers Lp(a) | Expensive (~$500/month US), injectable | Limited access, may require specialist |
| **Colchicine** | Anti-inflammatory (no cholesterol effect) | Proven CV event reduction (COLCOT, LoDoCo2), cheap, old drug | GI side effects at higher doses | Available |
| **Low-dose rosuvastatin** | HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor | Plaque stabilisation, anti-inflammatory, proven event reduction | IR worsening, muscle effects, liver enzymes | Available, cheap |

### Gut-Cardiovascular Connection (Jamnadas Framework — New)

Dr. Jamnadas presents evidence that gut-derived endotoxins (LPS from bacterial wall products) entering the bloodstream through intestinal permeability directly create small dense LDL particles and drive coronary calcification. He states the highest coronary calcium scores he now sees are in patients with "bad guts," not smokers.

**Direct relevance to Ben:** 2023 microbiome showed elevated zonulin (intestinal permeability), elevated ETEC (pathogenic bacteria), elevated methane producers, elevated beta-glucuronidase, and low secretory IgA. The gut protocol work (antimicrobials, enzyme support, zonulin reduction) is now reframed as cardiovascular medicine, not just digestive symptom management.
